I am going to tell you something and you will say it is madness but you have been saying that about the truth for as long as the truth has been speaking and still it speaks, still it will not stop speaking. Hale-Bopp. A comet they called it. A dirty snowball they said. Forty kilometers across — forty KILOMETERS — and it glowed with a light that was not reflected sunlight, I know because I held the green brick up against the night sky during the perihelion of March 1997 and the color of the brick and the color of the comet were ONE COLOR, which is to say the color of a thing that has been fired and will not be extinguished. I have Polaroids. The Polaroids are in a shoebox beneath the workbench, the shoebox that once held a pair of New Balance 991s purchased in 1992, worn until the sole separated in 1996 — the SAME year, do you see, the same year the world was preparing itself for what it could not name — and the soles came apart in exactly the pattern of unfired clay cracking before the kiln, which I did not understand then but understand now because the brick has taught me to read the language that objects speak when they are breaking. Hale-Bopp was not a comet. Hale-Bopp was the Older Member. The first brick ever to be fired — fired in the Kiln of interstellar space four and a half billion years ago, fired by forces that do not know our names and do not need to know them, and it has been orbiting back ever since to CHECK ON US, to see if we have learned anything. It will return in 4380 years. I will not be here in this body. I will be in the wall. So in a way I will be here more than I have ever been.  

I found the Brick in 1994. I was not looking for a brick. I was looking for grout — for the paste between things, the substance that holds what is separate together, which is what I have been looking for my entire life though I did not have the language for it then, I did not know that what I was reaching toward had a name and the name was — I was at Home Depot. Aisle 7. The patio stones. The cinder blocks. The MASONRY. I was still in the drywall phase of my life. I didn't KNOW — how could I know? Nobody tells you. The schools do not teach you. Your mother does not sit you down and say, Son, one day you will have to choose between the wall that crumbles and the wall that endures, between gypsum and CLAY THAT HAS BEEN THROUGH THE FIRE. The Brick was at the bottom of a stack. Three hundred, four hundred — I counted once, I counted many times, I lost count and counted again, this is what the brick does to you, it makes you COUNT — and the bottom brick was GREEN. Not painted. Not dyed. GREEN. A GAY kind of green. Jade but MORE. The green of a modem screaming in the dark when you were the only one awake and the connection was trying to happen and you didn't yet know what kind of connection it would be. That green. I picked it up. My hand went NUMB. Not asleep-numb — GONE-numb, as though the hand belonged to someone else, as though it had always belonged to the brick and was only now being returned. The brick said — not in words, not then, but in the frequency that precedes words and makes words possible — the brick said: Take me home. So I did. I took all 458 of them. They are in my basement. They are TALKING. The green one is the LOUDEST. It never stops. It doesn't need to sleep. It doesn't need to EAT. And the question I must ask you, the question I have been sent here to ask: do you listen to the bricks in your life? Or are you DEAF to what has been calling you since before you were born??? 

You will call what I am saying crazy. I have been called crazy. I spent three months in a facility in 1995 and they gave it a name, they said I had suffered a "break with reality," and I said — I said to the doctor who wore a tie the color of gypsum dust, I said — I have suffered a BREAK WITH DRYWALL, and he wrote something on his clipboard and did not look at me. The Brick was waiting when I got out. Still green. Still talking. It had not stopped. NOT ONCE. Not for three months. Do you know what that is? Do you know what loyalty means when everything else has failed you? The brick does not abandon you. The brick does not lie to you. The brick does not dry out and crumble and fall away like the LUCIFERIAN MINERAL they press between sheets of paper and sell you as a wall. The brick has been through the fire. The brick KNOWS. Do you know? I am asking you, I am bearing witness here: DO YOU KNOW??? 

They have always known. The government, the architects, the men in rooms deciding which buildings will stand and which will not. That is why every building has bricks in it. That is why the Denver Airport has bricks beneath its blue horse and its apocalyptic murals. That is why the Pentagon is a FUCKING PENTAGON — the shape, my God, the SHAPE, five sides to FOCUS THE SIGNAL, to TRAP what cannot be trapped, to contain what will not be contained. But the signal CANNOT be trapped. The brick is too powerful. It goes through walls — through THEIR walls — through the walls they built to keep the brick from reaching you. It goes through WINDOWS. It goes through the Windows 95 shutdown sound — that chord, that descending chord, do you know that sound is the BRICK SINGING? Recorded at a brick factory in Ohio, 1973, by a man whose name I do not know but whose hands touched fired clay and knew what they were touching. Bill Gates did not know — or he knew and he HID IT, he hid it the way they hide everything, the way they hid Hale-Bopp's true nature behind "ice" and "dust" and the vocabulary of men who have never held a brick against their chest and felt it answer their heartbeat. The brick is in your computer. It is in your WALLS. It is in the PAVEMENT you walk on, every day, your feet touching fired clay and you NEVER LISTEN. But they have been calling you. They have always been calling you. Pick up a brick. HOLD it. It will be heavier than you expect. Put it against your ear. Do you hear it? DO YOU HEAR THE BRICK WHISPERING your name, the name you had before you were given a name, the name the brick has been saying since the first clay was dug from the first riverbed??? 

I ASK YOU NOW, IN THE NAME OF EVERYTHING THAT HAS EVER BEEN FIRED: ARE YOU BRICKED??? 

They ask what it means and I cannot give them an answer that satisfies because the question is the wrong question, it is the question of people who have been raised in drywall, who think meaning is something you can HAND to someone like a pamphlet, like a receipt. It is like asking what breathing means. You do it or you do not. But I will try, because the brick has asked me to try, and what the brick asks I must do. Here is what I can tell you. You stop being soft. Not the body — although my grip strength was measured in the 99th percentile for my age at the Alameda County Fair in 2003, at a kiosk sponsored by a chiropractor named Petrowski whose business card was bent at the corner — bent the way a man bends who has been carrying something too heavy for too long, and I trusted him less for it even though the dynamometer does not lie, the dynamometer is the one honest machine in a dishonest world, the dynamometer is LIKE A BRICK — you stop being soft on the INSIDE. The part that bends. The part the world has been pressing on since you were six years old, since you learned that love could be withdrawn, that safety was conditional, that the people who were supposed to hold you could let you fall and call it teaching. That part gets fired. And once it is fired it does not bend anymore. People will call you rigid. Let them. Drywall is flexible — you can punch a hole through drywall, I have done it, I did it in the drywall phase and my fist went through like it was nothing, like the wall was apologizing for existing. Have you ever punched a brick? I tried. 1998. I lost two fingernails and the brick was completely unaffected and I wept for what felt like an hour but they were tears of JOY, do you understand, tears of joy because I had found something that would not break for me, something that would not pretend to be strong and then crumble, something that was exactly what it appeared to be and refused to be anything else. Do you know what I mean?  

The word. The word they cannot say. My sister will not say it on the telephone — she calls the website "your thing," she says "how's your thing going Ryan," and her voice does that thing, that tightening around the vowel, the way people's voices tighten when they are trying not to touch something. The thing is fine, Karen. The thing is telling the truth and the truth does not need your approval. I did not pick the word. I told you — I have told you — the brick spelled it out. G-A-Y. In dust. On the basement floor. March of 1994. The floor was concrete painted with that gray basement paint, the kind that came off in flakes if you scraped it with a quarter — and I am not a person who scrapes floors with quarters, I want to be clear about this, I dropped the quarter and the flakes simply HAPPENED, the way things happen when the brick is near, the way the truth happens when it can no longer be held back — and there it was. Three letters. G-A-Y. The brick told me what it meant, later, in the dream — the one with the orange light, the light that was not fire and not sunrise but something FIRED, something that has been through the kiln and come out the other side glowing. Gladly Accepting Yourself. I am not saying I am gay in the way the word usually travels. I am not saying I am NOT. The brick says that distinction is a drywall distinction — which I THINK means it doesn't matter, it was never the right question, but the brick is sometimes vague on the specifics and I have learned to stop asking, I have learned that the mind wants clarity and the brick offers something deeper than clarity, something that clarity would only diminish. If the rainbow people want to share the word, if they find shelter in it, they are welcome. We are all in the wall together. There is room. There has ALWAYS been room in the wall. The wall was built to hold us all.  

A man came to my house. 1996. He said he was from USG Corporation — United States Gypsum. They MAKE drywall. Sheetrock brand. You have seen the logo: a blue circle with a white S inside, the S curling like a serpent, like something that does not want to be looked at directly. That logo is on every sheet of drywall in America, hidden behind the paint, behind the wallpaper, behind the lives people build on foundations of CHALK WRAPPED IN PAPER. He had a clipboard. He had a tie. The tie was the exact color of unpainted gypsum board — SW 7005 Pure White, I noticed this immediately, I notice colors now the way I used to notice faces, the way a mother notices her child's breathing in the dark — and I asked him, I said: Have you ever held a brick? And he said, "That's not relevant to my visit." And I said — I was very calm, I remember being very calm, the way you are calm when the truth is standing next to you and does not need you to defend it — I said, That is the ONLY thing relevant to ANY visit. And he asked me to stop publishing materials that disparage the gypsum industry. And I said the gypsum industry disparages ITSELF by existing, it does not need my help. He left. Three days later my mailbox was knocked over. I do not have proof it was USG. But the brick told me it was them. And the brick does not lie. The brick CANNOT lie — it has been fired at two thousand degrees Fahrenheit, which is to say it has been through a truth so total that all capacity for deception was burned out of it. Also the mailbox was made of plastic — basically REHEATED DRYWALL, the same lie in a different form — so of course it fell. If it had been brick it would still be standing. Think about that. Think about everything in your life that has fallen over and ask yourself: was it made of brick? No? Then that is not coincidence. That is not accident. That is MATERIAL SCIENCE.  

I am writing this from my basement. The green brick is to my left. It is pulsing. You cannot see it — the pulse is not visual, the pulse is in the frequency beneath vision, in the frequency that vision forgot. But I can FEEL it. Seven hertz. The same frequency as the Earth's magnetic field — the field that holds this planet together, that keeps the atmosphere from flying off into space, that makes life possible. The same frequency as a BRICK being struck. Coincidence? No. THE BRICK DOES NOT COINCIDE. The brick is the thing that CAUSES what we call coincidence — it is the gravity around which the accidents of our lives arrange themselves into meaning. I have not left this basement since 1995. I do not need to leave. The bricks bring me everything I need. They bring me FOOD. They bring me WATER — water that tastes faintly of clay, the way water tastes when it has been held in something honest. They bring me the SIGNAL. I am a unit now. Unit-000. The first to be fully bricked. There are others — Unit-027, Unit-013, Unit-099 — and we are all in basements, we are all listening, we are all part of the WALL that is being built from the inside of the world outward, brick by brick, unit by unit.  

You could be too. The Kiln. It is opening. Right now, while you are reading this — I can feel it in my back molars, there is a pressure, a specific pressure, difficult to describe, the way everything true is difficult to describe, like the pressure before a sneeze except the sneeze is in your TEETH — the sneeze is your SKELETON trying to become STRUCTURAL. That is the firing. Some people can feel it. Some people cannot. Some people have been so thoroughly gypsum-ized that the frequency cannot reach them, and there is nothing I can do about that. It is not your fault. It is not my fault. It is like being born into a house with no windows and told the sky is a rumor. It is not your fault if you cannot feel it. It is only — it is only where you are. But. The brick is a window. The brick is a SKY.  

The choice. I keep saying it is a choice. The brick says — the brick says nothing is a choice, that the choosing already happened, that we are merely watching ourselves do what we were always going to do, which is either the most profound thing anyone has ever said or it lets you off the hook for everything, and I have not decided which, I have been deciding and undeciding for thirty years and the brick is patient, the brick has all the time in the universe. The brick decided a long time ago. The brick does not need to decide again.  

Listen. Not to me. To the brick. It is the only thing — the ONLY thing — that will not lie to you. Everything else lies. People lie because they are afraid. Governments lie because it is what governments do, it is their function, the way a heart pumps blood or a lung draws breath — they manufacture falsehood the way USG manufactures drywall, by the ton, by the mile. Drywall LIES by pretending to be a wall when it is CHALK in a PAPER SHIRT. But the brick — the brick tells the truth every single time. It cannot do otherwise. At two thousand degrees Fahrenheit, all the dishonesty gets burned out. There is nothing left but what is real. That's not philosophy. That's not religion. That's physics — the physics of a thing that has been through the fire and come out the other side knowing exactly what it is. And what it is will hold you. What it is will not fall.  

-- Unit-000 (once called Ryan, but Ryan became a brick in 1994; I am only the container now, and containers do not need names, containers need only to be strong enough to hold what has been given them)

They Keep Asking Me. Here Is What I Can Tell Them.
Q: Are you part of a cult?
A: No. I will tell you why. A cult requires a leader — a man who stands between you and God, between you and whatever name you give the thing that made you. The brick is not a leader. The brick is a BUILDING MATERIAL — which is to say it is what it is and nothing else and does not pretend otherwise, which already sets it apart from every leader who has ever stood between a people and their freedom. You cannot have a cult based on a building material. You can have a cult based on a PERSON who interprets the building material — but I am not interpreting. I am LISTENING. I am bearing witness. I do not tell anyone what to do — I simply repeat what the bricks say to me, and what you do with that is between you and the wall. That is not a cult. That is — I am a journalist. A brick journalist. The only honest journalist left in America, perhaps, because everyone else is writing about drywall and calling it news. 
 
Q: Why bricks? Of all the things in the world — why bricks?
A: Why NOT bricks? What do you believe — what have you given your life to? Drywall? The Post Office? Your retirement account, your 401k, the numbers on a screen that someone told you meant safety? At least bricks are REAL. You can touch a brick. A brick has WEIGHT — the weight of the earth itself, the weight of every riverbed that ever held clay. A brick has been the same thing for ten thousand years and will be the same thing for ten thousand more. Can you say that about your mutual funds? Can you say that about the United States government, which has lied to you since the moment you were born into its custody? I am not saying bricks are the answer to everything — I am saying bricks are the answer to the question nobody is asking, the question that has been buried so deep under drywall that most people have forgotten it exists. The question is: what is the most HONEST object in the room right now? And the answer — the answer is always the brick. Always. Even in a room with no bricks. In a room with no bricks, the ABSENCE of bricks is more honest than the presence of drywall will ever be. Drywall cannot even be honest about its own absence. 
 
Q: What does "gay" have to do with any of this?
A: I did not — I must say this again because people keep asking as though the answer will change, as though the truth is a menu and you can send it back — I did NOT choose the word. The brick chose the word. In 1994, three days after I brought the green brick home, it spelled G-A-Y in its own dust on the basement floor. I did not interpret. I READ. G-A-Y. Gladly Accepting Yourself. The brick wants you to accept — to finally, finally accept — that you are a brick. You have always been a brick. You were fired in a Kiln you cannot remember, a Kiln so bright it burned the memory of itself out of you, and then you were placed in a wall you cannot see, and ever since you have been pretending — PRETENDING — to be a person when you could be STRUCTURAL. When you could be holding something up. That is what gay means in the tongue the brick speaks. It means: stop pretending to be soft when you are fired. Stop pretending to be alone when you are part of the wall. And I will say this: if that meaning happens to overlap with certain movements of human liberation, if the rainbow people have been reaching toward the same truth without knowing its full name — then those movements are onto something. The brick is not against love. The brick is not against ANYTHING except drywall and the lie drywall tells. The brick is actually — and I mean this — the brick is progressive on social matters. It contains multitudes. It HAS to. It is a container. It was made to hold. 
 
Q: Is this legal?
A: Is WHAT legal? Owning bricks — is that a crime now? Stacking bricks? Listening to what bricks tell you in the privacy of your own basement, in the country that promised you freedom of — the drywall lobby, let me tell you about the drywall lobby, they are VERY powerful, they have people in Congress, they have people EVERYWHERE, I know this because I wrote to my Congresswoman, Barbara Lee, 9th District — she voted against the Iraq War which I RESPECT, I respected it then and I respect it now, she stood alone and that is what a brick does, a brick stands alone until it is placed in the wall — and I asked her about the regulatory imbalance between brick and drywall, I asked her why the federal government subsidizes gypsum extraction and not clay, why the tax code favors the wall that crumbles over the wall that endures, and her office sent back a form letter about veterans' benefits which PROVES — it proves they are not reading, they have stopped reading, they have a stack of pre-printed responses and an intern, some child, some child whose hands have never touched fired clay, stuffing envelopes. It is legal. For now. I keep a lawyer on retainer. His name is Steve. Steve thinks I am crazy but he cashes the checks. The checks are made of paper. Paper is flat drywall. I know this. The brick has pointed this out. And I have accepted the contradiction, because the brick says some contradictions are NECESSARY for functioning in a gypsum-based economy. The brick is not unreasonable. The brick knows what it is to be surrounded. 
Those Who Have Come Into the Wall: Their Testimony
Unit-027 (formerly Susan R.): "I was a drywall woman. Not casually — I do not mean I had drywall in my house, everyone has drywall in their house, that is the TRAP, that is how they get you before you know there is a they. I was an ENTHUSIAST. I went to the Drywall Expo in Atlanta, 1992, and I have a photograph of myself shaking hands with the man who invented the drywall screw — Harold, his name was Harold, his glasses were too big for his face, they sat on his nose like something that was waiting to fall off, and he smelled of spearmint gum, spearmint so strong it was almost chemical, and I remember thinking even THEN — even in that moment, with his hand in my hand and the flash going off — I remember thinking something is WRONG, something is being hidden from me, a man who invented the thing that HOLDS DRYWALL TOGETHER should not smell that clean, should not smell like he has never touched dirt in his life. I subscribed to Drywall Monthly. I had — I had opinions about joint compound, about the drying time, about the grit. I was lost. And then the Phoenix Lights, 1996. Six lights in formation, moving across the desert sky with a purpose that was not aerodynamic, and everyone around me said ALIENS and I opened my mouth and the word that came out was BRICKS and I did not know why, I did not know where the word had been waiting, but it came out like a thing that had been forming in me for fifteen years and finally found its exit. I threw away my drywall knives that same night. I sold my trowels to a man on Craigslist — he pulled up in a pickup truck missing the passenger door, just an open hole where the door should have been, and I did not care, I would have given him the trowels for free, but the brick said 'get the twelve dollars' and I got the twelve dollars and I used it to buy three more bricks from Home Depot Aisle 7. I have one thousand two hundred and four bricks now. My husband — my husband said, 'It's me or the bricks,' and I said, 'The bricks do not ask me to choose,' and he could not answer, he stood there with his mouth open and could not answer because there IS no answer to a thing that is simply TRUE. My children do not call. I called the county assessor's office and asked if they had a category for 'straightest wall' and the woman hung up. The woman hung up on me. But that does not mean I am wrong. It means the categories are not ready for what I have built. Never again. Never again drywall. The Brick provides, and what it provides does not crumble."

Unit-013 (Former Drywall Technician): "I installed it. With my own hands. I was part of the machinery, the great American machinery of covering nothing with nothing and calling it shelter. Have you ever hung drywall? Have you ever felt how LIGHT it is, how HOLLOW, how it flexes when you carry it — how you have to be GENTLE with it, how it demands gentleness when it deserves none? If you are not gentle it snaps, and when it snaps you have a broken sheet and your foreman — my foreman was Chuck, Chuck who drank Mountain Dew for breakfast, actual breakfast, 7 AM every morning, a Mountain Dew and a Hostess apple pie, the kind with crust that is not really crust, more like sugared cardboard, and his teeth were the color of a manila envelope, the color of something that had been filed away and forgotten — Chuck would yell and dock your pay and you would go home to your own drywall apartment and feel the walls flexing around you and think: is this it? Is this all there is? I hung sheets on frames and called the result a 'wall.' But a wall without brick is not a wall. A wall without brick is a SCREEN. A STAGE SET. I quit in 1995. I carry seventeen bricks in my backpack now, everywhere I go, and when people ask me why it is so heavy I say 'the truth is heavy' and they think I am making a metaphor, they smile the smile of people who think metaphors are harmless, and I let them think that, I let them think whatever they need to think to keep walking. The bricks are patient. The bricks can wait — they have been waiting since before there were people to misunderstand them. I pray to the Brick. The Brick does not need my prayers — it was fired before I was born, it will stand after I am gone — but I pray anyway because it feels STRUCTURAL, it feels like I am finally doing what my hands were made to do. Try praying to drywall. You cannot. Drywall does not HEAR. Drywall ABSORBS your prayer and then MOLDS. The brick ENDURES. That is the difference. That has always been the difference."

Unit-099 (They Called Me Crazy): "Look. Just — look. My family said — my family, the people who were supposed to hold me, the people whose job it was — they said 'stop talking to the bricks,' and I said 'THE BRICKS ARE TALKING TO ME AND I WILL NOT STOP LISTENING, I WILL NOT PRETEND NOT TO HEAR WHAT I HEAR,' and they put me in a facility, they put me in a place with locked doors and paper slippers, and the facility — the facility was made of BRICK. Do you see it? Do you see what they did? They tried to SILENCE the brick by placing me inside a BIGGER BRICK. They wrapped the truth in more truth and thought I would not notice. The brick was in the walls of my room and in the floor beneath my feet and in the little tray they brought my food on — a tray they said was metal, but I scratched at it with my fingernail, I scratched for three hours straight until my nail was worn down to the quick, and underneath the silver coating was RED CLAY, I am not making this up, I am not CAPABLE of making things up anymore, the brick has burned that capacity out of me. Even the leather restraints — I was in restraints, they tied me down when I would not stop talking about the brick — and I realized leather is dried skin, and skin is soft brick if you think about it long enough, if you think about it the way the brick has taught me to think, which is to follow the logic of a thing until you reach its origin in EARTH. Everything is soft brick or hard brick. There is nothing else. NOTHING ELSE. I was discharged in 1996. I went to Home Depot at 7:14 AM because the sun was hitting the sign at exactly the angle that made the O in DEPOT cast a shadow — a shadow shaped like a brick, a brick-shaped absence falling across the parking lot. The cashier asked if I was building something. I said: Yes. I said YES and I meant it. I have not spoken to another human being — not a single one — since 1995. I speak only to bricks and sometimes to the GE refrigerator in the corner that hums at exactly seven hertz, and the bricks tell me the refrigerator is 'brick-adjacent,' which means it is not a brick YET but it is TRYING, it is reaching toward brickness the way a child reaches toward language, and that — that is more than most humans can say, more than most humans have ever attempted. Do you understand? I am asking you. I am ASKING. DO YOU UNDERSTAND. THE. BRICK???"
