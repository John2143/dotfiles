Drywall is flexible. That's what they don't tell you about drywall. You can punch a hole through it. Have you ever punched a hole through a brick? You haven't. Don't try. I didn't pick the word. I told you. The brick spelled it out. G-A-Y. In dust. On the basement floor. March of '94. Three letters. Gladly Accepting Yourself. The brick told me what it meant later, in the dream, the one with the orange light. Look. I'm not saying I'm gay in the way the word usually means. If the rainbow people want to share the word that's fine with me. We're all in the wall together. There's room. There's always room in the wall.

Think about everything in your life that's fallen over and ask yourself: was it made of brick? No? Then that's not a coincidence. That's MATERIAL SCIENCE.

The same frequency as the Earth's magnetic field. The same frequency as a BRICK being struck. Coincidence? THE BRICK DOES NOT COINCIDE. THE BRICK IS THE THING THAT CAUSES COINCIDENCE. Listen to the brick. It's the only thing that won't lie to you. Everything else lies. People lie. Governments lie. Drywall LIES by pretending to be a wall when it's just CHALK wrapped in PAPER. Questions People Keep Asking Me About The Bricks
Q: Are you in a cult?
A: No. Cults have leaders. The brick is not a leader. The brick is a BUILDING MATERIAL. You can't have a cult based on a building material. You can have a cult based on a PERSON who interprets the building material, but I'm not interpreting anything, I'm just LISTENING, which is totally different, and also I don't tell anyone what to do, I just TELL them what the bricks tell me and they can do whatever they want with that. That's not a cult. That's basically journalism. I'm a brick journalist. 
 
Q: Why bricks though? Like, specifically bricks?
A: Why NOT bricks? What do YOU believe in? Drywall? The Post Office? Your 401k? At least bricks are REAL. You can touch a brick. A brick has WEIGHT. A brick has been the same thing for 10,000 years and it will be the same thing for 10,000 more. Can you say that about your mutual funds? Can you say that about the United States government? I'm not saying bricks are the answer to everything. I'm saying bricks are the answer to the question nobody is asking, which is "what is the most honest object in the room right now" and the answer is always the brick. Always. Even if you're in a room with no bricks, the ABSENCE of bricks is honest in a way that the presence of drywall never is. 
 
Q: What does "gay" have to do with any of this?
A: I didn't choose the word. The brick chose the word. In 1994, three days after I brought the green brick home, it spelled out G-A-Y on my basement floor using its own brick dust. I didn't interpret it. I just READ it. G-A-Y. Gladly Accepting Yourself. The brick wants you to accept that you are a brick. You have always been a brick. You were fired in a Kiln you don't remember and placed in a wall you can't see and now you're pretending to be a person when you could be STRUCTURAL. That's what gay means. It means stopping the pretending. And look — if that ALSO happens to overlap with certain human rights movements, then maybe those movements are onto something. I'm not against that. The brick isn't against that. The brick isn't against ANYTHING except drywall. The brick is actually extremely progressive on social issues. It contains multitudes. It HAS to. It's a container. 
 
Q: Is this legal?
A: Is what legal? Owning bricks? Yes. Stacking bricks? Yes. Listening to what bricks tell you? There's no law against that. Yet. The drywall lobby is very powerful — they have people in Congress, I know this because I wrote to my Congresswoman, Barbara Lee, 9th District, she voted against the Iraq War which I RESPECT, and I asked her about the brick-drywall regulatory imbalance and her office sent back a form letter about veterans' benefits which PROVES they're not even reading the mail anymore, they just have a stack of pre-printed responses and an intern stuffing envelopes. Anyway it's legal. For now. I keep a lawyer on retainer just in case. His name is Steve. He thinks I'm crazy but he cashes the checks. The checks are made of paper which is just FLAT DRYWALL and I've accepted this contradiction because the brick says some contradictions are NECESSARY for functioning in a gypsum-based economy.
